Positive Effects of Exercise on The Brain



  1. Improved Energy Levels

    2. Have you ever felt too worn out to study effectively the night before the big test? Some students will attempt to resolve this problem with some coffee or tea. Caffeine will unfortunately take awhile to take its effect and possibly lead to getting less sleep. It is recommended to do some pushups, jumping jacks or even a quick jog. It may sound odd, but getting the body moving can revitalize your state of mind much faster than a cup of coffee would. Exercise is a natural way to wake the body and mind up.


  2. Improved Emotional State

    3. Exercising has more benefits that go beyond physical. It is proven to help improve one’s state of mind. Exercising releases endorphins throughout the body. These endorphins reduce overall stress levels and can lead to happiness. Due to this, it is a good idea to exercise before class or a big study session. It can help with clearing any stress from the mind beforehand.


  3. Improved Memory

    4. That's right, exercising can improve one’s memory. Studies show that physical exercise releases proteins to the brain that can help improve both memory and cognitive performance. So, if you feel like it's difficult to study effectively or take tests, exercising can help boost your performance in these areas.


Along with exercising regularly, you should also be eating a well balanced diet! Here’s the top 6 food choices you should be eating, as recommended by doctors:


  1. Eating plenty of vegetables, fruits, and whole grains.
  2. Choosing fat-free or low fat dairy products, fish, poultry, beans, nuts, and vegetable oils.
  3. Limiting saturated and trans fat intake, such as fatty meats and full-fat dairy products.
  4. Limiting drinks and foods that contain added sugars.
  5. Restricting sodium intake to less than 2,300 milligrams per day — ideally 1,500 mg daily— and increasing consumption of potassium, magnesium, and calcium.
  6. High fiber foods are also crucial for keeping the heart healthy.

Eating healthy also reduces risk of cancer, better mood, improved gut health, improved memory, weight loss, diabetes management, strong bones and teeth, and getting a good night’s sleep!
